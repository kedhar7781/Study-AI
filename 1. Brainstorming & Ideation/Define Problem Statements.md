# Phase 1: Define Problem Statements

This document outlines the core user needs, challenges, and target problem statements that the **StudyAI** application is designed to solve.

---

## 1. Context & User Profile

Our target audience is modern students, self-taught learners, and professionals preparing for exams:
- **Pain Point A: Content Overload**: Students are overwhelmed by 100+ page syllabus textbooks and lengthy study slides.
- **Pain Point B: Passive Reading**: Highlighting text is a passive study method that leads to poor long-term retention.
- **Pain Point C: Lack of Custom Testing**: Students struggle to find targeted practice exams covering their specific syllabus.
- **Pain Point D: Poor Organization**: Tracking what topics are mastered versus what needs review is chaotic without active logs.

---

## 2. Core Problem Statements

We formulated three primary problem statements to guide our system design:

### Problem Statement 1: High-Yield Information Synthesis
> *"How might we help students synthesize long PDF notes into high-yield, structured highlights (definitions, tricks, examples) so they can absorb material in 10 minutes rather than 2 hours?"*

### Problem Statement 2: Interactive Active Recall
> *"How might we convert static notes into active learning resources (3D flashcards and quizzes) that dynamically test understanding and track correct rate progress?"*

### Problem Statement 3: Adaptive Revision Tracking
> *"How might we detect a student's weak subjects based on quiz history and automatically suggest structured daily revision schedules?"*
